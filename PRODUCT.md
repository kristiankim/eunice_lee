# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is prospective adult therapy clients in New York and New Jersey, including people seeking support for anxiety, depression, relationship concerns, life transitions, immigration or adjustment, self-esteem, and anger management. Visitors may be uncertain about beginning therapy and need to understand Eunice’s approach, qualifications, logistics, and fit without feeling pressured. Some prospective clients prefer Korean-language support. The experience must work equally well on desktop and mobile web.

## Product Purpose

This personal-brand and informational website introduces Eunice Lee and Journey 2 Grow Therapy, establishes professional credibility, answers the practical questions that commonly delay therapy, and gives prospective clients a low-pressure way to request a free 15-minute consultation. Success means a visitor feels informed, reassured, and comfortable enough to submit a consultation inquiry.

## Positioning

Eunice offers professionally grounded, collaborative individual therapy with the practical flexibility of online or in-person sessions, licensure across New York and New Jersey, and care available in English and Korean.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: request a free 15-minute consultation.
- Secondary path: learn about Eunice’s approach, credentials, specialties, fees, insurance, and session options on the Home page.
- The line a visitor should remember after 10 seconds: “Professional therapy for anxiety, relationships, and life transitions.”
- Belief ladder: Eunice understands concerns like mine; she is qualified and experienced; her approach feels collaborative and practical; the location, language, format, and insurance options could work for me; a free consultation is a safe, manageable first step.
- Proof currently on hand: LCSW licensure in New Jersey (44SC05929300) and New York (096779); MSW from Columbia University (2016); ten years in practice; Certified Clinical Trauma Professional certification (2022); English- and Korean-language care; online and in-person availability; insurance acceptance. Testimonials, press, affiliations, and other third-party proof are not currently available and must not be implied. Add them only after Eunice supplies and approves them.

## Brand Personality

Calm, sincere, and professionally grounded. The voice should be clear, warm, private-practice scale, and reassuring without sounding clinical, overly casual, or sales-driven. The intended emotional result is quiet confidence: visitors should feel respected, unhurried, and able to take a small next step.

## Anti-references

The site should not resemble a cold medical portal, generic wellness or spa brand, glossy large-agency production, or conversion-heavy sales funnel. Avoid vague self-care language, exaggerated claims, decorative clinical imagery, trend-driven therapy clichés, and any presentation that feels impersonal or overproduced.

## Design Principles

1. Build trust before asking for action: answer emotional and practical questions in a calm sequence before repeating the consultation CTA.
2. Make beginning feel manageable: present the consultation as a brief, low-pressure first conversation rather than a commitment to treatment.
3. Pair warmth with evidence: use sincere language and welcoming imagery while keeping credentials, licenses, logistics, and boundaries easy to verify.
4. Respect vulnerable attention: keep navigation, copy, forms, and motion clear, focused, and free of urgency tactics.
5. Design mobile as an equal experience: preserve hierarchy, readability, form usability, and emotional tone at narrow widths rather than treating mobile as a compressed desktop layout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain keyboard usability, visible focus states, readable text and placeholder contrast, responsive typography, semantic structure, clear validation and crisis guidance, and reduced-motion alternatives. Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning. Language about therapy must remain inclusive and understandable, and bilingual care should be represented accurately without implying translated content that has not been approved.
